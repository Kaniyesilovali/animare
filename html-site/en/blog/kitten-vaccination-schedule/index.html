Kitten Vaccination Schedule: Everything You Need to Know

18 June 2026 · Animare Veterinary

Why Do Kittens Need Vaccines?

Kittens are born with antibodies passed on from their mother (maternal immunity). However, this protection begins to wane around 6–8 weeks of age, before the kitten's own immune system is fully developed. Vaccination during this window protects against several serious and potentially fatal diseases.

Core Kitten Vaccination Schedule

  • 8–9 weeks: First combination vaccine — Panleukopenia, Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus (FVRCP / "3-in-1")
  • 12 weeks: Second dose of FVRCP
  • 12–16 weeks: Rabies vaccine (legally required in the TRNC)

Annual booster doses maintain immunity once the core series is complete.

Which Vaccines Are Compulsory?

In the TRNC, rabies vaccination is a legal requirement for cats. In addition, three core vaccines are recommended for all cats:

  • Panleukopenia (feline distemper): A highly contagious virus with a high mortality rate.
  • Rhinotracheitis (FHV-1): A herpesvirus causing upper respiratory tract disease.
  • Calicivirus (FCV): A virus responsible for mouth ulcers and respiratory infections.

What About Feline Leukaemia and FIV?

For cats that go outdoors or have contact with other cats, your vet may also recommend the feline leukaemia (FeLV) vaccine. There is currently no approved vaccine for FIV (feline immunodeficiency virus); the most effective protection is keeping cats indoors and away from unknown cats.

What to Expect After Vaccination

Mild lethargy and reduced appetite for a day or two after vaccination is normal. However, if you notice severe facial or neck swelling, breathing difficulties, or collapse, call us immediately on +90 533 844 91 92.

Keeping Up with Adult Cat Vaccines

After the initial series, adult cats benefit from an annual veterinary check-up and the appropriate booster doses. Some vaccines may be given every three years — your vet will advise accordingly. Contact us to book an appointment at our Gönyeli clinic.

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age should my kitten have its first vaccine?

The first vaccination is typically given at 8–9 weeks, when maternal immunity begins to wane and the vaccination window opens.

Which vaccines are compulsory for cats in Lefkoşa / Nicosia?

Rabies vaccination is a legal requirement in the TRNC. The core combination vaccine (FVRCP) covering panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus is recommended for all cats.

My kitten doesn't go outside — does it still need vaccines?

Yes. Many feline viruses are highly resilient and can be carried indoors on clothing or shoes. Vaccination is important even for indoor-only cats.

What should I do if my kitten seems unwell after vaccination?

Mild lethargy for 1–2 days is normal. If you notice severe swelling, breathing difficulties, or changes in consciousness, call our clinic immediately.
