When to Wait — and When to Act Immediately

Pets are instinctively good at hiding pain and discomfort. By the time symptoms become obvious, the situation is often already serious. If you notice any of the following signs, don't wait — delay can sometimes mean the difference between recovery and a fatal outcome.

10 Emergency Signs

1. Difficulty Breathing

Rapid open-mouth breathing, wheezing, or an inability to catch breath may indicate a lung, heart, or airway problem. This requires immediate attention.

2. Seizures or Loss of Consciousness

Trembling, convulsions, sudden collapse, or an unresponsive pet may signal poisoning, epilepsy, or a serious neurological event.

3. Inability to Urinate (especially male cats)

A cat straining repeatedly in the litter box but producing no urine is a genuine emergency. A blocked urethra can cause kidney failure and death within hours.

4. Bloated or Rigid Abdomen

In large-breed dogs especially, a swollen hard belly combined with restlessness and drooling may indicate gastric dilatation-volvulus (GDV). This condition can be fatal within hours without surgery.

5. Uncontrolled Bleeding

Wounds, or bleeding from the mouth or nose that cannot be stopped within 5 minutes, require urgent veterinary care.

6. Repeated Vomiting or Bloody Diarrhoea

A single episode of vomiting can be normal. But three or more episodes, or any instance of bloody vomit or diarrhoea, may indicate poisoning, parvovirus, or organ failure.

7. Sudden Inability to Walk or Paralysis

Dragging a leg, sudden inability to stand, or weakness in the hind legs may point to a spinal, vascular, or neurological problem that requires immediate assessment.

8. Eye Injury or Sudden Vision Loss

Eye trauma, sudden blindness, or severe discharge should be assessed without delay. Eye conditions deteriorate rapidly and can lead to permanent damage.

9. Suspected Ingestion of a Toxic Substance

Chocolate, grapes, onions, ibuprofen, rodenticides, certain houseplants — if you suspect your pet has eaten any of these, don't wait for symptoms to appear. Call immediately.

10. Difficulty Giving Birth

If active labour has been ongoing for more than 30 minutes without a puppy or kitten being delivered, veterinary intervention may be needed.

What to Do on the Way to the Clinic

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do if there's a pet emergency on a Sunday or at night in Lefkoşa?

Call our clinic on +90 533 844 91 92. We will direct you to the nearest on-call veterinary service.

My cat is straining in the litter box but can't urinate. Is this an emergency?

Yes, this is a genuine emergency. In male cats especially, a blocked urethra can cause kidney failure and death within hours. Call immediately.

My dog ate something and is vomiting. When should I go to the vet?

If you suspect a toxic substance was ingested, call immediately — whether vomiting has started or not. Three or more episodes of vomiting or any bloody vomit also requires urgent assessment.

My pet fell but seems fine. Do I still need a check-up?

Yes. Internal bleeding and organ damage may not be visible externally. A veterinary check-up is strongly recommended after any high fall or severe impact.
