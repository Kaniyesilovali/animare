Everything You Need to Know About Your Dog's Vaccination Schedule

20 May 2026 · Animare Veterinary

Why Is Vaccination So Important?

Vaccination is the most effective and cost-efficient way to protect your dog against many deadly and contagious diseases. A correct vaccination schedule dramatically reduces the risk of parvovirus, distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, and rabies.

Puppy Vaccination Schedule

Puppies are ready for their first vaccines when the maternal immunity starts to wane, usually around 6–8 weeks:

  • 6–8 weeks: First combination vaccine (DHPPi)
  • 10–12 weeks: Second DHPPi + Leptospirosis
  • 14–16 weeks: Third DHPPi + Leptospirosis + Rabies

Once this core series is complete, annual booster doses maintain immunity throughout adulthood.

Annual Check-ups for Adult Dogs

Adult dogs benefit from a yearly veterinary visit and the appropriate booster doses. Some vaccines (such as rabies) are subject to local legal requirements that vary by region.

What to Watch After Vaccination

Mild lethargy or reduced appetite for a day or two is normal. If you notice severe swelling, breathing difficulties, or changes in consciousness, contact our clinic immediately.

Conclusion

Keeping vaccinations up to date protects your dog's health as well as other animals in the community. Contact us to book an appointment.

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age should I give my puppy the first vaccine?

The first combination vaccine (DHPPi) is typically administered at 6–8 weeks. By this time, maternal immunity begins to wane and the vaccination window opens.

Which vaccines are mandatory for dogs?

In North Cyprus, rabies vaccination is a legal requirement. Additionally, the combination vaccine against parvovirus, distemper, and hepatitis (DHPPi) is among the core protective vaccines.

What should I do if my dog acts sick after vaccination?

Mild lethargy or loss of appetite for 1–2 days is normal. However, if you notice severe swelling, breathing difficulty, or changes in consciousness, call our clinic immediately.

I missed my adult dog's vaccination schedule — what should I do?

For overdue vaccinations, your veterinarian will create a new schedule based on the current immune status. No matter how long the gap, it is always possible to restart vaccination.
